Closing keynote

Hrvoje Benko

Hrvoje Benko is a research science manager at Oculus Research in the field of Human Computer Interaction. Prior to his current role, he was a researcher at Microsoft Research. His interests span augmented and virtual reality, interactive projection mapping, haptics, new input form factors and devices, as well as touch and freehand gestural input.

He is passionate about creating highly interactive and adaptive real-time systems that highlight the core innovations while at the same time surprising and delighting the user.

He has been active in the Human-Computer Interaction research community and already served on various program committees (CHI, UIST, SIGGRAPH, ISMAR, ITS/ISS). He was the general chair for ACM UIST 2014 and the program chair for ACM UIST 2012 and is currently on the ACM UIST steering committee and on the advisory board for SmartHaptics. He also is the Information Director and Associate Editor for ACM TOCHI journal, the premier journal in my field.

At Microsoft, he worked on several projects that were released as Microsoft products or as open-sourced projects, including Microsoft Touch Mouse, Windows Touch Visualizations, and RoomAlive Toolkit. He also was one of the founders of studio99, a magical group of artists-in-residence and scientists working together to inspire new ways of thinking at Microsoft.
